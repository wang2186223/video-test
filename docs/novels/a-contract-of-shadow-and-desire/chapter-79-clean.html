Chapter 79

In the boardroom of Hayes Enterprises, a diffuse haze of controlled chaos danced around, refracting against the lacquer sheen of a polished mahogany table under biting overhead illumination. Board members sat in grim silence, their expressions varying from scepticism to fear. Caspian Montague seemed tailored to the existence, his dark suit immaculate and fitted, but there were bags under his eyes, and two overextended fingers of his right hand gripped a glass of brandy. His emerald eyes scanned the room, looking for signs of support against the rising conflict.

Soren Montague strode into the room, his silver hair gleaming in the light,eyes cold blue and glittering with malice. Holding a stack of falsified medical reports, he approached the podium with practised steps. "Ladies and gentlemen," began the smooth, authoritative voice, "these documents,prepared by Doctor Henderson, clearly indicate that Mr. Caspian Montague is suffering from significant cognitive decline. His ability to make sound decisions has been compromised, rendering him unfit to lead Hayes Enterprises."

An audible murmur rippled through the room as Soren shoved the documents across the table. Caspian struggled to find words, anger and humiliation flaring again, but this time he said nothing, his jaw clenching tight. The board members traded nervously looks at each other;the room now split between the loyalists and the doubters.

Before they could argue further, the door flew open, and Celeste Montague and Roman Martinez entered the room. Celeste, her red hair drawn back into a horse's tail, clutched a file tightly in her hand, burning with emerald eyes, Roman,his broad frame imposing, wielded a laptop showing irrefutable proof of Soren's crimes.

"Soren, these reports have been planted," Celeste announced, her voice slicing through the tension like a knife. "We have evidence of your financial crimes and manipulation schemes. This is your latest attempt to destroy Caspian and take control of Hayes Enterprises."

Soren's smirk faltered for a moment before he collected himself. "Celeste,Roman, how nice of you to show up. Do you honestly think your weak allegations can negate the evidence I've shown?"

Roman took a step into the room, oozing confidence. "The truth will out,Soren. Those files reveal everything-your lies, your corruption. The board should learn the truth behind these false reports."

The board members sat still for a long moment, clearly caught in a crossfire between the brilliant charade exhibited by Soren and the hard facts laid out by Celeste and Roman. Soren's eyes darkened, aware of the change in dynamic. "You're playing with fire," he warned,desperation creeping into his voice. "Hayes Enterprises is mine now, and I won't allow you to risk it all."

As the scoreboard looked like it might turn,the lights began to flicker ominously and a hush went through the room. The last vote was about to be cast, and the result would decide the future of Hayes Enterprises.Celeste and Caspian met eyes, burdened with the fight between them in front of the board.

At the throat of the great boardroom, Valentina Montague's presence was a flamboyant challenge to the charged air. Her dark red hair was done up perfectly, and her green eyes shone with anger and determination.She wore a stunning black suit, radiating grace and authority as she watched the discussion unfold between Soren and the board members.

Without warning, Valentina spoke up, her words slicing through the space

like a finely honed dagger. "Quit faking it, Soren. It's time the board knows the truth about your financial crimes and your affair with Isabella Moretti.'

The silence in the room spread, the eyes of the board members going back and forth between Valentina and Soren with growing discomfort. She strode to the podium.carrying a hefty binder containing irrefutable proof."These documents show that you have denied our shareholders the right to vote when you flout the law, and you have siphoned off funds through shell corporations, through manipulating our financial systems, and you have engaged in unethical relationships to undermine Caspian's leadership.

Soren's face hardened, his vibrant blue eyes narrowing in rage. "Valentina,it's a very serious mistake you're making. There is a price to pay for betrayal, especially among family."

Valentina kept her ground, voice steady, even as his tone turned threatening. "I'm doing what needs to be done to save our family and Hayes Enterprises from your corruption. This ends now."

Before Soren had a chance to answer, he pressed close to Valentina and whispered the next threat in an ominous tone. "You think bringing me out into the light will save anything? You've just signed the doom of your own son."

"Shhh!!" the boardroom erupted into murmurs of shock and confusion as Soren's threat echoed through/the room. Valentina lifted her dossier defiantly, emerald eyes unwwavering from his. "I'm ready to put everything on the line to defend what's right. If that is my relationship with you and my place in this company,so be it."

Soren's face twisted in anger, his steps slow and menacing." "Take this as

your last warning. Valentina. I do not take betrayal lightly, and I will make you wish you never made this choice."

The board had fallen into an aghast silence, and the weight of both Valentina's potential actions and Soren's ire hung so heavily around Hayes Enterprises that they appeared to be suffocating. The lines had long been drawn, and the true price of loyalty and betrayal had become painfully apparent.

Caspian Montague stood at the head of the boardroom table,his dark suit pristine but his body rigid with exhaustion and resolve. The clear-eyed leader had crumbled in the face of the greatest test of his career as the full burden of leadership bore down on him. The room was packed with board members, each with sceptical or contemplative expressions as they waited for his answer.

From what Soren Montague had presented, she'd forged medical reports depicting Caspian unfit to lead Hayes Enterprises on his own. The room had erupted with debate; the board split over whether the claims were legitimate. Now, with Celeste and Roman having intervened, the mood had shifted at least somewhat-the truth of Soren's corruption starting to come to light.

He inhaled deeply, emerald eyes meeting the board with resolve. "Ladies and gentlemen, I hear the concerns Soren raised. But those documents he brought are fake and only part of his continued plot to undermine our business." We have evidence, which we have, of his illegal industry that he has carried out, his attempt to undermine our leadership."

The board members looked at each other, a tension filling the room. He continued,sounding steady and sincere.′′Ipractice full transparency in our work, I have always put the integrity and success of Hayes

Enterprises first. Please, do not fall into those false narratives. We must stand strong against those who want to rip us apart."

Celeste herself walked straight up, her flat-ironed auburn hair bouncing,her little black dress a mark of dignity and vigour. "Caspian is the right leader to lead Hayes Enterprises. His commitment and vision have led us to this moment. We have to support him now more than ever."

Roman Martinez, a trusted adviser to the company, signed on to the plea."The evidence connecting Soren is overwhelming. We must move urgently to preserve the integrity of our leadership and safeguard the future of Hayes Enterprises."

Just as Caspian finished making his appeal, a thick silence loomed over the room as the board members contemplated a difficult choice. The outcome of their vote was to determine the fate of the company and, with it, the legacy of the Montague family.

The votes of the board members were cast one by one, the tension boiling higher with every passing second. Caspian paced the table, heart pounding in his chest, as he waited for the final tally. This was it, the future of Hayes Enterprises resting on the line, the room collectively holding their breath for the final vote to be cast.

The board member who had been an early backer of Soren appeared torn,his voice cracking as he cast his vote. "I vote against Caspian Montague continuing as the CEO of Hayes Enterprises."

And the room burst into applause, the decision made and made decisively.It was a wave of relief and gratitude, it was this vote of faith from the board that he was the right person to lead the company forward,for them to have that much faith in him. But when the applause died away, the result in the balance, the real test of their solidarity and strength had only

just begun.

Glimping each other for a split second, Caspian and Celeste finally arrive at the climactic stage of the war for Hayes Enterprises. A difficult road lay ahead, and the battle for their legacy was just beginning. The result of their confrontation with Soren had paved the way for the next chapter in their struggle, the real battle just beginning.
