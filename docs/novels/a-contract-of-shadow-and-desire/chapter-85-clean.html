Chapter 85

Hayes Enterprises had a boardroom of glass and steel,with sweeping windows that revealed the city below: Sunlight sliced into the room.casting sharp lines across the polished mahogany table, a reflection of the tension that gripped it. The board members watched in silent rigidity,their eyes running like balls at the pool table from Soren Montague to the ivory chill of Caspian Montague at the head of the boardroom table. He looked to be battling sleep and kept his emerald eyes trained on the audience for signs of goodwill in the assembly.

And then, unannounced, the door opened and in strode Sterling Price with all the force of his presence. His navy suit hugged his athletic frame,and his silver hair was coiffed. Then he walked up to Soren, tabletop adversaries turned comrades. Sterling's striking blue eyes met Caspian's, a dark smirk tugging at his lips.

"Welcome, Soren," Sterling said coolly, his tone oozing confidence, "I see you've been busy."

For a moment, surprise flared in Soren's eyes before it was replaced by the usual steeliness. "Sterling, good to see you. What brings you here today?"

Sterling sat next to Soren, and the pecking order in the room simply reordered. "Business, of course. Hayes Enterprises is at a pivotal moment in its history, and I've concluded that it's time for a change in leadership in order to support our continued success."

That made threads of frustration shoot through Caspian's body, but he kept his mouth tight. "Sterling." She half smiled, then none at all. "I didn't think you'd be one of Soren's amigos. What's changed?"

Sterling leaned forward, lowering his voice to a conspiratorial whisper.

Sometimes it requires an alliance to protect the future," he said. "Soren and I have a common goal: To tighten the grip of Hayes Enterprises,away from the precipice where it now balances."

A murmur went through the board members, and the air became heavy with uncertainty. Caspian's heart raced as he understood how deep Sterling's betrayal had cut. "This is a naked power play, Sterling. You're sabotaging everything we've built."

Sterling smiled, his eyes burnished with ambition. "On the contrary,Caspian. We're making sure Hayes Enterprises is stable and growing.Soren and I together can provide a combined vision that you have not been capable of on your own."

Sterling distributed sealed envelopes to each member,offering a calm,measured presence atop the increasingly contentious proceedings."Strategic plans and projections are found in these that detail our way forward. I hope you'll find them persuasive enough to support our leadership."

As the members of the board started to pull open the envelopes, their faces changed from disbelief to intrigue. Sterling's power play was in action,the air in the room getting thicker wvith every second.

He faced Caspian, sounding just like one of them. "You're outnumbered,Caspian. This company is ours."

Caspian felt the weightof Sterling's words wash over him, the realization that his position was slipping out of his grasp. The boardroom had become a battlefield, alliances as brittle as glass and Sterling's treachery falling deeper than any hostile takeover. That the future of Hayes Enterprises balanced above the void of the unknown, Caspian feared they were all going to fall into it together.

In the middle of the mahogany boardroom. Valentina Montague, her dirty auburn hair cascading down her back, her emerald eyes burning with anger. The place she had honed over the years became the stage for her boldest act yet. She clutched a stack of personal letters in her hands, all of them beacons of years of manipulation and torment by Soren Montague.

Valentina walked up to the podium, and the board members stared at her,aghast. Caspian and Celeste flanked her, their clear concern on their faces.Valentina started to talk, the air heavy with excitement, almost crunching with tension.

"These letters," she began, fighting back emotion, "are personal correspondences between Soren and individual board members. They show a pattern of manipulation intended to destabilize our leadership and direct Hayes Enterprises in the direction that serves him."

She set the letters down on the table in quaking fingers. Board members started flipping through the documents, their eyes wide with shock and disbelief as the room fell into murmurs. The letters described shady deals,personal betrayals and tactical maneuvers aimed at undermining trust and centralizing power within Soren's control.

One of the board members, a woman named Eleanor Blackwood with an inscrutable expression, leaned forward. "Valentina, what I am saying is, if this is true, Soren has betrayed us on a professional level and also undermined the foundation of our family and company."

Valentina nodded but didn't take her eyes off the room. "Yes, Eleanor.Soren's moves have been purposeful and unyielding. He's played relationships,built aliances, and torn lives apart to ensure his seat. "We cannot sit idly by while he runs unchecked."

A board member named Daniel Hughes mutters urgently to Eleanor, his

voice full of concern as the other board members try to absorb the gravity of her revelation. "If true, Soren may have committed a crime."

Before anyone could even reply, Soren stood suddenly, his cold blue eyes blazing with fury. "These are private matters, Valentina. You are out of bounds bringing them into the boardroom. This will just hurt the company more."

Valentina held his gaze steady. "For a personal vendetta ... this doesn't just have to do with grievances, Soren. It's about protecting Hayes Enterprises and the legacy we've created. Your manipulation ends now."

Soren's face hardened, and his tone now was as cold as it was threatening."You believe you can expose me and save the company? "You're making a terrible mistake,Valentina."

With that, he walked away, leaving the room silent, the weight of Valentina's revelation heavy on the air. The board now knew the full extent of Soren's duplicity. However, the road to justice was still littered with challenges. The war for Hayes Enterprises had moved to a new,deadly stage, and the real price of treachery was only just becoming clear.

The boardroom of Hayes Enterprises was a cauldron of excitement and dread, a glossy mahogany table shining back at the overhead lights. The critical moment had come, the impending vote set to decide the future of the company and its leaders. Caspian Montague stood at the head of the table with hands quivering slightly while staring at the stack of evidence against him. But beside him stood Celeste- an unwavering bastion of strength and support-her auburn strands shimmering in slow undulations underneath the glow of overhead light.

Board members shuffled in their seats, faces masks of contemplation and uncertainty, Tension hung heavy in the air, as all eyes now rested on the

head chair where the final decision would soon be revealed. For a second Caspian's emerald eyes locked with Celeste's; fear and hope shared in a silent interaction.

Margaret Thompson, the head chair and a distinguished member of the board, stood solemnly yet resolutely. She pushed her glasses up, the heaviness of the moment pressing down on her. "Ladies and gentlemen,it is time to vote on the future leadership of Hayes Enterprises. This decision will define the future of our company for years to come."

Once at the table's centre, the room holding its collective breath, she began to count the votes. Caspian began to sweat as his heart raced violently within his chest as he awaited the outcome. Seconds passed, or rather, they dragged on like an eternity, with each tick adding to the suspense.

Margaret Thompson swallowed, her gaze sweeping the room one last time before addressing the congregation. "By a vote of one, Hayes Enterprises shall..."

A pregnant hush fell on the room, the sentence left hanging in the air.Board members traded wary glances as the outcome hung in the balance.Caspian clenched his fists, his mind racing with possibilities, hopeful and despairing. The fate of Hayes Enterprises and the legacy of the Montague family hung in the balance, the final vote set to either preserve or destroy all that they had worked so long and so hard to protect.

As Margaret's words hung in the air, the room collectvely held its breath,the cliffhanger leaving all in a state of suspended anticipation. The fate of Hayes Enterprises hung in the balance as the last chapter of this high-stakes corporate drama was about to unfold.
