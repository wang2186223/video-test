Chapter 44

In the kitchen, Reese asked a servant to brew soup for Malcolm. Alyssa walked by at this moment and said."Can you take a look at why the flowers I planted died? I watered them just yesterday."

Sandra was a bit hesitant and said, "But I'm still brewing soup here. Can you wait?"

"I need those flowers for someone. It's my best friend's birthday in acouple of days, and I promised to give her this potted plant."

Alyssa forcefully dragged Sandra to the yard,and Sandra took a look.

"You might have watered them too much." "No. I didn't. They were perfectly fine yesterday.Besides, with this weather for the past two days,if I didn't water them, they would have died in no time."

Alyssa bent down and examined the flowers closely.

"Go to my room and bring some fertilizer. Let's give it a try. If it still doesn't revive in another two days, then I have no other options."

Sandra was not just a servant at home. She also took care of the Flynn family's plants and flowers. Alyssa got up and smiled, saying, "Alright, I'll go get it.Sandra, please continue to take a look."

Sandra was concerned about the soup in the kitchen and said, "Then please hurry."

Alyssa hurried towards the living room and passed by the kitchen, where she saw the soup on the stóve. She remembered what Reese had said to her earlier and couldn't help but sneer.

Just because she was beautiful, why didI she have the right to boss her around? Since she entered the Flynn family. Malcolm rarely spoke to her. Even when she asked him to accompany her, he would say he had no time. Yet, he spends his days with this annoying little girl.

As these thoughts filled her mind, her growing sense of injustice felt like an expanding balloon about to.burst. The anger in her eyes intensified.

Once Reese leaves the Flynn family, theirhousehold can revert to how it used to be.

Sandra waited outside for Alyssa for a while,but when she didn't come out, Sandra decided to check on Malcolm's soup in the kitchen. She entered the kitchen and saw that the water was about to boil dry, so she quickly turned off the stove and used a strainer to filter the residue of the soup into a bowl.

Alyssa came running down with fertilizer in her hands: "Sandra, where are you?"

"I'm here." Sandra came out of the kitchen carrying the soup. "You go ahead and spread the fertilizer,no need to water it. I need to bring this soup upstairs."

"Alright, now that you've explained it, I understand.You go do your thing."

Alyssa took the fertilizer and her gaze swept over the bowl of soup as she walked away.

Sandra approached the bedroom door and knocked on it. "Sir. I have finished preparing the soup."

"Come in," Reese's voice came from inside.

Sandra placed the soup on the bedside table,and Reese asked one more question.

"You've been guarding this soup, right?"

"Yes, no problem."

"Then please leave for now."

After Sandra left, Reese was about to bring the soup to Malcolm, but when she caught a smell of it,she hesitated. There was something off about the scent.

Malcolm noticed her unusual expression and asked,"Is there something wrong with the soup?" Reese couldn't be sure.

"In theory, there shouldn't be anything wrong, but I just have this feeling that something's not quite right How is your relationship with Sandra?"

"She has been with the Flynn family for a long time and has always been good to me. I don't think there should be a problem."

Besides, theperson who wants to harm him is not here. The Flynn family mansion only consisted of two sisters and their grandfather, and they wouldn't want to harm him.

Reese's tensed brows eased slightly upon hearing this."I must have been overly cautious. This soup is something I developed in the past few days, and it works both externally and internally." Seeing how much she cared and researched about his injured legs, Malcolm's gaze towards her became even gentler.

"Alright then, if I can really walk again, Honey, you've played a significant role. Do you need any rewards as my wife?"

Reese didn't need anything. She chuckled lightly,"What can you give me? I married into the Flynn family, and I don't have to worry about food or clothing. You even gave me a card before, so now I'm a wealthy woman."

Although she hadn't used a single penny from the card Malcolm gave her, it was better to accept and let him feel at ease, rather than letting him know she didn't need it and have him find other ways to give her money.

Malcolm took hold of her chin with his fingertips, a smile in his eyes. "Can I offer myself as a reward?"

Reese treated it as a joke. She self-deprecatingly said,"I dare not let you sacrifice yourself. Your family has two sisters, and I can't bear parting with them."

As soon as this matter was mentioned, a commotion could be heard outside, and the room door was forcefully pushed open, accompanied by a strong gust of wind. Reese frowned, a hint of displeasure passing through her eyes.

The people who came were not only Everly and Alyssa, but also Grandpa Flynn and Vincent. Vincent had just landed and returned to the Flynn family, yet he hadn't even had a chance to sit down before he heard Alyssa's scream from the kitchen.

As soon as Everly entered the room, she saw Reese holding soup in her hand, apparently preparing to feed it to Malcolm.

With a calm face, she quickly stepped forward and snatched the soup bowl from Reese's hand. A little splashed out, scalding Reese's hand, and her eyes were already filled with a storm.

"Miss Everly, is this your idea of manners?"

Everly stared at the bowl of soup and coldly snorted,"Reese, this time I want to see how you justify yourself. I have long said that she has sinister intentions and must have ulterior motives for coming to our house."

Reese disdainfully sneered, and her cold and deep gaze swept over Everly. Her tone was indifferent and calm, but it sent chills down Everly's back, freezing her triumphant expression. ."Then, Miss Everly, do tell, what do I gain from your family?"

Everly arrogantly lifted her face.

"Does that even need to be said? You and Malcolm are legally married now. If he dies unexpectedly, his property becomes yours. Everyone knows the company is under Malcolm's name."

The implication couldn't be any clearer.

Facing Everly's question, Reese showed no signs of panic on her face. Instead, she turned the tables and questioned Everly in return.

"You accuse me of plotting against Malcolm, but where is the evidence?" "The evidence is in this bowl of soup."

Reese suddenly realized why the aroma seemed so strange earlier. It turned out someone had added other substances to it.

She smirked, leisurely stood up, and walked in front of Everly.

"Then how can you be sure that there's poison in here? If Malcolm really dies, you won't let me go either. Am I that stupid?"

Malcolm sat on the bed, not intending to help Reese,as he wanted to see how she would handle her predicament.

If it was their first meeting,he might have kindly assisted her. However, after spending time together, he increasingly felt that this girl was no ordinary'person. In terms of both thinking and language, she was not inferior to anyone present.

Moreover, she always managed to surprise him. This time, he also wanted to see how she would resolve her own crisis in the face of so many people's questioning.

Everly pulled Alyssa over and said, "Alyssa, you tell them."

Alyssa said, "When I went to the kitchen just now, I found some leftover powder in the trash can. I picked it up and was wondering why Sandra's soup had that kind of colored powder. Just then, Vincent came back and I showed it to him. That's when I realized it was a poison. If someone consumes it, they will become disoriented and paralyzed, and their organs will fail within seven days, leading to death."

Upon hearing Alyssa's words, Grandpa Flynn, who had experienced many trials and tribulations.showed a fearful expression on his face.

"There is poison in this soup. Reese, are you trying to kill my grandson?"
